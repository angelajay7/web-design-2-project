Welcome To Ging's Garden

This website is an amalgamation of all of my favourite things related to the Pokemon franchise. In my 24 years on earth, I have played my fair share of Pokemon generations, my childhood favourite being Sapphire on my Gameboy Advanced. Eventually I graduated through to Pearl, White, and Soul Silver on my DS lite. If you can't already tell, I had an older sibling who got first pick when a new generation duo was released. After a long hiatus, I was recently gifted a switch lite and have been able to play and enjoy Pokemon again through Scarlet. Ever since I was young, beating the game with the strongest possible Pokemon has never been my goal. I enjoy catching cute Pokemon, growing berries, and enjoying all the visually pleasing pixels the games have to offer. arrow_drop_down

Little Cuties

These Pokemon hold the spot for my top three cutest in my personal opinion. These are the Pokemon that I have loved since I was little and I rank a solid 5/5 stars on the cutie ranking system. arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Jigglypuff

An expected but honest first place has to go to Jigglypuff. As a child I would spend hours catching as many as I could so that I would have entire PC boxes filled with just Jigglypuffs. Round, pink, curly front bang, sings you to sleep. There is nothing I would change about this little Pokemon. arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Vulpix

Vulpix was the Pokemon I wanted most to exist in real life. It is all around perfect with it's cute tail and white belly. In Pokemon Sapphire I would spend much of my time on Mt. Pyre searching for this little Fire-type cutie. I also think the evolution, Ninetails, is very special as well. arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Togepi

Togepi is another expected cutie for obvious reasons. Of course Misty was an icon to me growing up, and I often longed to cradle and go on Adventures with a small Togepi just like she did. The almost 80's like design on this Pokemon's shell... arrow_drop_down

Big Jambas

These Pokemon are what I consider to be the top 3 biggest and best Jambas in my experience. These Pokemon are large, round, and often strong. I also rate them 5/5 stars on my Big Jamba scale. arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Snorlax

Snorlax is the quintessential Big Jamba. If you were confused about the definition before, just take a look at this Pokemon, and you will understand. A description of Snorlax from the Ruby and Sapphire Pokedex is: "Snorlax's typical day consists of nothing more than eating and sleeping. It is such a docile Pokémon that there are children who use its expansive belly as a place to play." arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Swampert

Swampert is the final evolution of Mudkip, which was my starter Pokemon when I played Sapphire. I think this is a big factor to why it is in my top three Biggest Jambas. Swampert and I have been through everything together, and I feel like it would protect me from danger in real life situations by sheilding me with it's big body. Those gills and that stance are signs of a true Big Jamba. arrow_drop_down

catching_pokemon Nidoqueen

Nidoqueen is the smallest of the Big 3 Jambas, but a Jamba nonetheless. She is one of my favourites because I like that they intentionally made the female version blue, and the male one pink. Not that that has anything to do with Jambaness, but it is a detail I enjoyed as a child and made Nidoqueen stick out in my memory all these years. The Pokedex description of Nidoqueen from Sapphire is " Nidoqueen's body is encased in extremely hard scales. It is adept at sending foes flying with harsh tackles. This Pokémon is at its strongest when it is defending its young." arrow_drop_down

Other things that I think are Cute

This section consists of other aspects of the Pokemon franchise that are not actual Pokemon, but I still think deserve recognition for being cute. These events, objects, and attributes are memorable pieces of different games that I loved. arrow_drop_down

Decorated Mail

Pictured is one of the decorated letter pages that you can send as mail in Generation III pokemon games. This one is titled Tropic mail and features Bellossom. I loved to write different messages and give them to my Pokemon to hold. arrow_drop_down

Secret Bases

The Hoenn region has 75 different locations where you can make and decorate your own Secret Base. To be able to enter these hideouts, you must have a Pokemon that can use th TM Secret Power. Bases can be decorated to your liking with a limit of 16 decorations. I have made an example of a possible set up, pictured here.
