Welcome To Ging's Garden

This website is an amalgamation of all of my favourite things related to the Pokemon franchise. In my 24 years on earth, I have played my fair share of Pokemon generations, my childhood favourite being Sapphire on my Gameboy Advanced. Eventually I graduated through to Pearl, White, and Soul Silver on my DS lite. If you can't already tell, I had an older sibling who got first pick when a new generation duo was released. After a long hiatus, I was recently gifted a switch lite and have been able to play and enjoy Pokemon again through Scarlet. Ever since I was young, beating the game with the strongest possible Pokemon has never been my goal. I enjoy catching cute Pokemon, growing berries

Little Cuties

These Pokemon hold the spot for my top three cutest in my personal opinion. These are the Pokemon that I have loved since I was little and I rank a solid 5/5 stars on the cutie ranking system.

An expected but honest first place has to go to Jigglypuff. As a child I would spend hours catching as many as I could so that I would have entire PC boxes filled with just Jigglypuffs. Round, pink, curly front bang, sings you to sleep. There is nothing I would change about this little Pokemon.

Vulpix was the Pokemon I wanted most to exist in real life. It is all around perfect with it's cute tail and white belly. In Pokemon Sapphire I would spend much of my time on Mt. Pyre searching for this little Fire-type cutie. I also think the evolution, Ninetails, is very special as well.

Togepi is another expected cutie for obvious reasons. Of course Misty was an icon to me growing up, and I often longed to cradle and go on Adventures with a small Togepi just like she did. The almost 80's like design on this Pokemon's shell, along with the cute way it walks around is more than enough to earn a spot on my top 3 cutest little Pokemon list. /p>

Big Jambas

These Pokemon are what I consider to be the top 3 biggest and best Jambas in my experience. These Pokemon are large, round, and often strong. I also rate them 5/5 stars on my Big Jamba scale.

Snorlax is the quintessential Big Jamba. If you were confused about the definition before, just take a look at this Pokemon, and you will understand. A description of Snorlax from the Ruby and Sapphire Pokedex is: "Snorlax's typical day consists of nothing more than eating and sleeping. It is such a docile Pokémon that there are children who use its expansive belly as a place to play."

Swampert is the final evolution of Mudkip, which was my starter Pokemon when I played Sapphire. I think this is a big factor to why it is in my top three Biggest Jambas. Swampert and I have been through everything together, and I feel like it would protect me from danger in real life situations by sheilding me with it's big body. Those gills and that stance are signs of a true Big Jamba.

Nidoqueen is the smallest of the Big 3 Jambas, but a Jamba nonetheless. She is one of my favourites because I like that they intentionally made the female version blue, and the male one pink. Not that that has anything to do with Jambaness, but it is a detail I enjoyed as a child and made Nidoqueen stick out in my memory all these years. The Pokedex description of Nidoqueen from Sapphire is " Nidoqueen's body is encased in extremely hard scales. It is adept at sending foes flying with harsh tackles. This Pokémon is at its strongest when it is defending its young."
